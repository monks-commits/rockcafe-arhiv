Alexander Ljubchenko - one of the Best jazz guitarists of Europe



Alexander Ljubchenko - About

Alexander Ljubchenko was born in 1952 in Dnepropetrovsk. He began to do his first steps into the music since he was six years old. When he was eight it was the first time he took a guitar to his hands. He have got an excellent private education.

Using of many different musical cultures is typical for creation of Alexander Ljubchenko. There are European classic tradition, Indian classic music, flamenco, Slav native music (Russia, Ukraine, Poland, Bulgaria, Serbia), jazz, blues, rock, intonations of East. A great influence to Alexander as a guitarist have been done by Bamey Kessel, Jim Hall, Joe Pass, Jimi Hendrix, John McLaughlin, Paco de Lucia, Eqberto Gizmonti, Ralf Towner, Ravi Shankar, John Williams, Django Reinhardt.

His professional working start when he was 16 years old. Since 60’s he performed at the jazz-festivals. It was many times that Alexander have been recognized as the one of the greatest jazz guitarists of the country. He played with many famous rock and jazz bands in 70’s. He performed as accompanist for chamber and pop singers.

Since 1976 he prefers untraditional styles of music. He works a lot as a composer («Guitar Almanac #1 — 1986»), «Musical Ukraine», «Musical life», 3 encyclopedias: «Anthology of Soviet Jazz», «Encyclopedia of young musician», «Jazz in USSR», republican and regional press line of those years, diplomas, prizes).

In 1981 Alexander Ljubchenko took a part at the first guitar festival in the country where he have been recognized as the best in two nominations — Flamenco and Jazz.

From 1982 up to 1986 Alexander Ljubchenko have been a leader of the famous jazz quartet «Credo», he performs with it having a great success at many jazz-festivals, the festivals of a guitar music and on tour around the country.

In 1986 Alexander Ljubchenko forms a trio — Igor’ Sobko — a bass guitar/ Igor’ Kolisnichenko — drums/ Alexander Ljubchenko — a guitar. The trio plays the music full of spirit of traditional jazz, rock-vanguard and folk music roots. The trio went on with the great success tours all around the country, had a great success at modern music’s audience, but unfortunately in 1988 the trio breaks apart… — «We just got a feeling that repeat it all over» — Alexander Ljubchenko quoted.

1988 — Alexander Ljubchenko plays solo concerts.

In 1989 he have been invited to perform at the festival of Krakow dedicated the Beatles music in Poland. His performance have been broadcast all over the Polish television for many times. After that he had the performances in the line of «JnO-89» jazz festival in Wrotslaw, Poland. At the festival Alexander Ljubchenko performed in the stars’ line-up concerts («Jazz Forum» Poland and «Jazz Haus» Germany and others). At the festival Alexander got to know with famous American double-bass player composer Siron (Noris Jones) and having got an offer to play together he takes it. The same year Alexander Ljubchenko takes part in the 1st International festival of the Beatles music that took place in Dneprodzerzhinsk. At this festival he have been recognized as the best interpreter of the Beatles music and the best guitarist. In Wrotslaw, Poland there were a set of the concerts in duet with Siron. There was a recording in the television of Wrotslaw and broadcasting all over Poland.

In 1990 Alexander plays the concerts with a quartet «A Point of Unity» and in duet with Siron in Kreis theatre, Viden, Ausrtia and in Hebel theatre, Berlin, Germany. The press named Alexander Ljubchenko «Guitar Paganini». At the end of 1990 Alexander meets with the former bass player Igor’ Sobko. Their views to the music and performing came to the same point and they form up the duet «Brave New World». The music of «BNW» been drown to the Easternslav intonations and colors, the blues have been replaced with a Russian native romance. «BNW» takes part at the jazz festivals of Russia, Lithuania, having a great success at the audience owing to the beauty of music and virtuosity of performers.

In the Autumn of 1991 «BNW» become a sensation having perform at the International Moscow radio festival (its former was a famous musical critic and art-critic Dmitry Ukhov). «BNW» have been enthusiasts and initiators of forming the festival dedicated to Jaco Pastorius in Naberezhnye Chelny (ãðóäåíü1991 ð.). Television records the performance of «BNW» and broadcast it all over Ukraine.

In September — October of 1993 Alexander plays a set of concerts with a bass player Michael Krymov (the former bass player of «Credo») in Lintz, Austria. Republic television records the live concert of the duet and broadcast it in the TV-program «A Guitar Academy» (the only serious program dedicated to the guitar). At least for 20 times Alexander came up in this program for the period of 1992 — 1995 years. Dmitry Ukhov in his radio program «Jazz alarm-clock» (radio Mayak, Moscow) applied and referenced to the music and playing manner of Alexander Ljubchenko repeatedly.

In period of 1994–1995 years Alexander Ljubchenko turns to the teaching activity. He forms his own guitar school, invents the unique system of teaching playing guitar. He performs the solo concerts. Deeply involved in the Slav traditions, as the musical and a spiritual as well (the Orthodox music). Acting in duet with Sergey Makarov (double-bass).

1996 — 1997 — a period of solo performances. Recordings on the television. Playing in the festivals. Forming of the project «Super-Trio» — Alexander Ljubchenko — a guitar; Michael Krymov — a bass- guitar; Svyatoslav Jakovenko — the drums. Acting in a duet with Michael Krymov. Playing in a duet with Sergey Makarov.

October of 1997 — sensational performance in duet with Michael Krymov in the «Inter-Guitar’97» festival, Kiev. Recordings on the television.

1998 — 1999 — concert performing and teaching acting.

2000 — common touring with a jazz guitar star Alexey Kuznetsov (Moscow). Acting in duet with Sergey Makarov.

2001 — performing in trio — Alexander Ljubchenko — guitar; Igor’ Ljubchenko — guitar; Genady Klyujevsky — bass guitar. Performance at the International jazz-festival in Dnepropetrovsk.

2002 — acting in Spain. Cooperation with Karlos Benavent (the former guitarist of Paco De Lucia).

2003 — 2004 — soloist of the Philharmonic Society of Dnepropetrovsk. Concerts, festivals. Duet activity with Yury Piven’. An albums «Tonka Gorobyna» and «Vojin vich’nosti».

October of 2004 — the concert of Alexander Ljubchenko Trio — Alexander Ljubchenko — guitar; Vitaly Hrenov — drums; Jury Piven’ — electro-bass in the line-up of the festival «European jazz on the Dnepr».

It was an absolutely fantastic performance played by trio leaded by Alexander Ljubchenko, the one of the greatest European guitar players. A synthesized sound seemed to rip off the air, explosive passages and licks, subtlety rhythmical patterns, maximal giving the instrument’s sound closer ties with man voice intonations. Especially the audience felt it when Alexander start to sing up in unison with a guitar (composition «Klyuch» (’A key’), showing an excellent technique inherent in Balkan melos. Just outstanding action. What a charisma! Those who attended the concerts of John MacClauhlin’s or Paco De Lucia will get what it all about. High jazz level have been showed by a drummer Vitaly Hrenov from Moscow. Many from the audience would be amazed if only would know that the musicians haven’t rehearsed much together (just sound-check tuning). Yuri Piven’ left a pleasant impression too, the bright and aggressive manner of playing distinguish him. The result was an natural jazz-rock, the one that I haven’t heard from a festival scene for a long time.

D. Mushkin, «Dnepr Vecherniy» newspaper #169 (11034), 06.11.2004

On the 16-th of October 2005 he was awarded by the State Duma of Russia for the development of the culture values of Russia.
